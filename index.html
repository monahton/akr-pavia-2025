General Information

Welcome to the R Crash Course @UniPV – a hands-on, low-stress, high-impact introduction to data analysis for wet lab researchers who’ve never written a line of code (and aren’t sure they want to). We get it: you’re busy pipetting, staining, troubleshooting protocols, and trying to figure out how your Western blot turned into a Rorschach test. The last thing you need is to waste hours dragging and dropping data into Excel and hoping it behaves.

This course is designed to save your time (and your sanity). We’ll gently walk you through the basics of R – a powerful, open-source language that’s basically the Swiss army knife of data analysis. You'll learn how to tidy messy data, make beautiful, publication-ready plots, and do basic stats with confidence (and maybe even joy?). No previous programming experience is required – if you can Google your PCR troubleshooting, you can learn R.

By the end of this workshop, you’ll be able to write clean, reproducible code, produce customized figures that don’t make your PI cry, and understand what “ggplot2” is without assuming it’s a sequel. You’ll also leave with a mini R toolkit tailored to your lab life – and the confidence to explore more on your own.

Who: The course is aimed for wet-lab researchers with no coding experience.
Where: This training will take place online via Teams.
When: May 07 - 16, 2025

Requirements

Participants must have access to a computer with a Mac, Linux, or Windows operating system (not a tablet, Chromebook, etc.) that they have administrative privileges on.
They should have a few specific software packages installed (please check the Setup page)
